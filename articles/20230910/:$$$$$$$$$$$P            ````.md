# 2.背景介绍
## What Is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation and automated adaptation of human intelligence through machines. AI is defined as any system that mimics or exhibits characteristics of intelligent beings, such as human-level intelligence, reasoning, learning, problem-solving, creativity, and self-awareness. It involves designing software systems that interact with real-world environments to achieve specific goals within certain constraints.

In general terms, AI involves creating computational models capable of performing complex tasks by considering multiple inputs and producing accurate outputs. These models use various techniques such as machine learning, neural networks, pattern recognition, decision trees, and statistical methods to map input data to output decisions. These models learn from experience and can improve over time based on feedback received from their performance. To meet practical challenges associated with large scale problems, these models have been adapted to take advantage of parallel computing architectures. Thus, they can efficiently handle large volumes of data and provide solutions quickly.

Moreover, there exists a growing demand for advanced technologies like natural language processing (NLP), computer vision (CV), and deep learning (DL). These advancements leverage the power of modern hardware to create powerful AI systems that analyze vast amounts of data, extract insights, and make predictions. By integrating NLP, CV, and DL into a unified framework, organizations can build more comprehensive applications for predictive analytics and decision making.

Thus, AI provides a promising new paradigm for unlocking tremendous benefits in many industries across various domains such as healthcare, finance, transportation, energy, and so on. At the same time, it presents several challenges alongside its rapid development. Despite the advances made in recent years, the pace of technological progress is not keeping up with the pace of society's needs. Therefore, we need to stay ahead of the curve and seek novel ways to address the existing shortcomings and optimize our solutions. Moreover, as the field continues to evolve, ethical and legal considerations must also play an essential role in ensuring fairness, transparency, and accountability in AI. 

## Machine Learning

Machine learning is a type of artificial intelligence (AI) that enables computers to automatically improve their performance through the analysis of data. It consists of three main components:

1. Data collection
2. Algorithm selection and training
3. Performance evaluation

The process begins with collecting data which is then preprocessed to obtain useful features for classification or regression. Next, an algorithm is selected and trained on the processed data. The goal is to develop a model that accurately identifies patterns in the collected data without being explicitly programmed. Once trained, the model is evaluated to determine how well it performs on new datasets. If the model does not meet desired levels of accuracy, it may require retraining or refinement.

One of the earliest forms of machine learning was developed by <NAME> in 1959 while studying at Dartmouth College. He called his work "The Pursuit of Mathematical Knowledge," which evolved into the first successful application of mathematical optimization theory called “Learning” by the German mathematician Widrow Hoff. During the era of Shannon’s work, linear classifiers were used to classify binary objects such as digits and letters.

However, the limitations of linear classifiers became apparent when non-linear relationships between variables could exist in the dataset. To address this challenge, statisticians introduced techniques such as kernel functions, support vector machines, and decision trees. All these techniques helped to develop increasingly sophisticated models that could capture more complex patterns in the data. However, despite the breakthroughs achieved by these techniques, machine learning still faced several fundamental limitations. One limitation that remains crucial today is the lack of scalable solution that can handle massive datasets and deal with continuous variables. As a result, researchers continue to push the boundaries of machine learning by developing new techniques, specifically deep learning. Deep learning is a subset of machine learning that uses multi-layered neural networks to learn abstract representations of data. These models are able to solve complex problems with higher accuracy compared to traditional approaches.

Deep learning models often outperform linear classifiers but suffer from slower convergence due to the complexity of their architecture. Additionally, the underlying assumption of linearity becomes problematic when dealing with highly nonlinear data. Overall, machine learning has provided us with valuable insights into solving complex problems in numerous domains. Nevertheless, it requires careful consideration of both algorithmic complexity and applied engineering practices to ensure that the resulting solutions are robust, reliable, and effective.

## Deep Learning

Deep learning is a subfield of machine learning that focuses on artificial neural networks inspired by the structure and function of the human brain. Deep learning models are typically composed of multiple layers of interconnected nodes that apply transformations to the raw input data until a desired output is produced. In particular, deep neural networks (DNNs) consist of two types of layers:

1. Input layer: Consists of the input features to the network.
2. Hidden layers: Consist of intermediate processing units that apply linear combinations of the activations of neighboring nodes in the previous layer. Each hidden unit applies a non-linear transformation to its inputs and passes the transformed value to the next layer.

After passing through all the hidden layers, the final output is obtained via a softmax activation function that computes a probability distribution over possible classes given the learned representation.

Compared to traditional DNNs, convolutional neural networks (CNNs) are particularly effective for analyzing visual imagery and other multimedia data. A CNN comprises multiple convolutional filters that scan the input image and produce feature maps that are passed to subsequent layers for object detection or classification. Compared to traditional ANNs, CNNs have fewer parameters and require less computation per weight update, leading to faster convergence times.

Reinforcement learning (RL) is another exciting application area of deep learning. RL involves training agents to learn behaviors that maximize rewards over long periods of time, similar to animal training programs in nature. Unlike supervised learning where labeled examples are presented sequentially, RL agents interact with the environment in real-time and receive feedback that tells them whether actions taken resulted in positive or negative outcomes. Based on this feedback, the agent learns to modify its behavior accordingly. Reinforcement learning is particularly beneficial in robotics, gaming, and autonomous driving where an agent should adapt to its surroundings and learn to complete missions under uncertain conditions.

Overall, deep learning offers unprecedented potential for advancing artificial intelligence capabilities. However, the ultimate success of deep learning lies in achieving sufficient level of automation and generality required to replace entire industries. While deep learning has already enabled some groundbreaking breakthroughs, it will require rigorous testing and validation before it can be considered a panacea for the industry.
