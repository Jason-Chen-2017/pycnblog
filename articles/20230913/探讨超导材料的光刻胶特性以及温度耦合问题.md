# 1.简介
  

光刻胶（MOLEX）是一种耐磨性超导材料，在电子系统、电气系统及其互联系统中应用广泛。对于电子系统来说，MOLEX的光刻胶密度是电子基态库存的一项重要参数，也是电子集成度的一个重要指标；而对于电气系统来说，MOLEX的光刻胶耐压性能以及稳定性更为重要，主要用于连接器件与电缆等电力设备之间的连接，并且具有较高的能耗效率。由于MOLEX的材料性质和成分的特性，使得其能够承受高速的热传递，提高其耐磨能力。因此，如何设计有效的MOLEX电子设备将是当下研究的热点，也是本文所关注的问题。

临界电流是MOLEX光刻胶的重要性能指标之一，其定义为：在静止状态下，由于厚度限制导致的绝缘层阻抗在给定的压强下所释放出的电荷能量所能够达到的最高值。电流值越高，则表明材料能够承受的压力越大；反之，则表明需要增大材料厚度或改变结构才能获得更高的电流值。

另一个非常重要的性能指标是反应速度（Reactivity），反应速度反映了材料在压强变化时的响应能力。MOLEX光刻胶在不同温度下的反应速度差异很大，超过一定的压制阈值之后，就会发生光刻胶的断裂，出现相对晶格失真（Grain Distortion）。然而，要避免光刻胶的断裂或者提高反应速度仍然是一个挑战。

本文将通过分析光刻胶的厚度和自旋向量分布进行探索，来寻找合适的MOLEX光刻胶电子设备的设计策略，并针对性地提出一些优化方向。本文假定读者已经具备对电子学、材料科学和高性能计算的基本了解。

# 2.	基本概念术语说明
## 2.1 MOLEX
Micro-Ordinary Lithium Electrode material EXchange，即微型普通铝电极材料交换。是一种耐磨超导材料，由锡镉酸钠组成，具有强大的电导性、良好的导电性能，可作基底电路板、晶圆结构管道的电子芯片、环保用电器材料等用途。目前市面上主流的MOLEX种类有两种：

1. 普通电极材料：由LSC、MoS、SMX等三种金属基底成形，制成特殊的晶圆结构，可承受高电压，可提供高导通度，具有强大的高耐腐蚀性，因此应用于控制系统、热电偶、真空烟囱等领域。

2. 低密度电极材料：由LSC、MoS、SMX等三种金属基底，在一定条件下发生化学反应，产生新的MOLEX材料，具有较低的厚度和导通性，但采用了特殊加工手段，并非任意MOLEX材料都可以迁移到该类型。如同LPE、SiO2、EtO等材料一样，可用于阻止电流，提高抗干扰能力。



## 2.2 概念和术语
### 2.2.1 厚度 Thickness
厚度Thicknesses are the measure of how thick an object is. For example, in a film it refers to its physical width; in biology it measures the length of tissue that forms a cortex or cell. In plasma physics and high energy density physics, it refers to the characteristic extent of a magnetic field. A thin film can have a thickness up to several nanometers (nm), while a solid can be several micrometers long. The thickness of MOLEX films depends on their composition, application, and other factors such as temperature, humidity, degradation rate, and surface defects. 

厚度会影响MOLEX光刻胶的结构组织形式、功能、性能，它决定着MOLEX光刻胶的尺寸大小、熊熊燃烧后的凹凸程度以及闪烁的痕迹。通常MOLEX光刻胶的厚度以微米(μm)为单位，通常可以达到十微米以下，甚至不到100纳米。

### 2.2.2 自旋向量 Spin Vector
MOLEX光刻胶存在着自旋作用，可以通过改变自旋向量的方向来进行攻击。自旋向量又称自旋直角坐标系中的z轴。每个孔中都含有与其周围空间对应的两个自旋向量，即与正极相邻的负极和与负极相邻的正极所构成的两个自旋向量，通常取正极为动量守恒方程中的p+,负极为动量守恒方程中的n-.



### 2.2.3 配对 Ligand Pairing
Ligand pairing, also known as “reentrant” lithography or spin–lattice relaxation, is a method for patterning flexible organic materials by controlling their order parameter, usually via controlled axial movements of electron spins. Ligand pairing can either produce spatially distinct patterns or alternatively generate pseudo-random patterns using entanglement in the solvent phase. It has been widely used for various applications including electronic devices, printed circuit boards (PCBs), and optical fibers.

Ligand pairing involves controlling the motion of electron spin pairs along specific directions. This motion creates spatial separation between regions of equal charge density. By varying these control parameters one can create ever-changing shapes and configurations of the material under different conditions. Spontaneous ligand pairing mechanisms typically occur at low temperatures where the spin-orbit coupling constant becomes stronger than any transferable work function difference. However, there exist deleterious effects due to excessive polarization and interference resulting from poorly designed structures, incorrect regulation of the excitation process, and reduction in recombination rates. Therefore, careful design of both structure and control parameters is required to achieve the desired shape and behavior.

### 2.2.4 温度 Temperature
Temperature describes the degree of heat capacity and hence the resistance to heat transport. It ranges from absolute zero (-∞K) to sub-atomic values (10^5 K). At typical operating temperatures (usually about 400 °C or 1000 °F), the atoms present in most metals and semiconductors maintain relatively large internal degrees of freedom which allows them to absorb significant amounts of electrical energy without forming fermi levels. When external heating increases beyond this limit, some types of atoms become protected from evaporation and structural damage by negative charged spins and orbital interactions with free electrons. These protected regions of the material are called valence shells and consist mainly of noble gases like oxygen, hydrogen, helium, etc. Once electrons move into the valence shell they may scatter off to produce unwanted emissions, leading to increased environmental impacts.

The higher the temperature of the system, the faster the electrons diffuse through the material, reducing local heat content. Consequently, reduced mobility of electrons within the material results in a decreased ability of the device to conduct current and thus lower power output. To improve the performance of MOLEX light-emitting diodes (LEDs), researchers have focused on finding ways to minimize temperature-induced variations in electricity generation, especially when compared to competing technologies based on photodiodes and laser diodes.
