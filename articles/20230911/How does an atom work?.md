# 1.简介
  

宇宙中每个微小的粒子（atom）都被设计成同样的结构和特性。这些特性包括电子、质子等原子核所处位置的三维坐标、占据该位置的原子核、引力场对粒子的影响力。宇宙中的微观世界不断膨胀壮大，但如此复杂的结构却是如何形成的？在本文中，我们将探索原子核、电子及它们的互相作用产生的电子辐射。最后，我们将看看人类从本质上来说还是一个活生生的原子！ 

# 2. 基本概念与术语
## Atom（原子）
- A small chemical element that is the smallest building block of matter and consists of one or more protons, neutrons and electrons in a stable orbital arrangement called their valence shell. Each atom has three lone pairs of electrons, which carry energy from the nucleus to interact with its surroundings. In solid state, atoms have about two proton valence electrons and four neutron valence electrons per atom. An atomic nucleus can also emit light by exciting the electric field induced between it and other atoms or molecules, producing radiation called photons.

> 概括地说，原子是一个化学元素，它是一个最小的物质构件，由一个或多个质子、中子和电子组成，具有稳定的重叠轨道排列，称为电势带。每一个原子都有三个独自的电子双对键，它们从核中获得能量来与周围的环境相互作用。固体态下，原子核的两个电子氢原子和四个半中子电子，分别代表原子核的电荷数量。原子核也可以通过激发其他原子或者分子间引起的电场来发射光辐射，这就是用原子核激发出的电磁波叫做电离辐射。

- The term "atom" refers specifically to the most basic unit of structure, consisting of three fundamental forces: electromagnetic force, strong nuclear force, and weak attraction due to gravity. However, some materials (such as silicon) behave like atoms because they are magnetic and conduct electricity well enough for them to act as wires when exposed to air currents. The word "atomic" means in reference to this property.

> “原子”这个词特指构成世界最小单位的基本构件——三种基本力：电磁场力、强核力和弱吸引力。然而某些材料（比如硅）却可以像原子一样运动，因为它具备自旋，能够承受良好的电压，从而适合作为导线使用。“原子”一词还有一个重要意义，即它指的是共同拥有原子核、电子和离子的物质体系，具备某种属性的物质，其行为与质点的性质很像。

## Nucleus（核）
- The center of mass of an atom that makes up the atom's identity, located within the outer layer of the atom's periodic table column. It consists primarily of protons and neutrons, each of which occupies a different valance orbital type. Protons occupy odd valance numbers, while neutrons occupy even valance numbers. There are usually at least six protons in every atom and six neutrons for non-radioactive elements such as Hydrogen.

> 核是一种中心论模型，它确定了一个原子的身份，并保证了原子核内所有的质子与中子都处于不同的电势状态。质子处于奇数电势数字中，中子处于偶数电势数字中。对于氦原子等非毒元素来说，通常至少会有六个质子和六个中子。

## Electron（电子）
- Any of the three quark-gluon model fermions with identical properties and charge repulsive interactions. When charged, electrons compete with one another to keep themselves oriented along the z axis and form collectively known as the pion(π) meson. Pions are composed of three quarks and three gluons, with the fourth antiquark being produced in the decay process. These particles are responsible for the radioactivity of certain elements, including hydrogen and helium, but not carbon or oxygen. They can be created either through fusion reactions or nuclear fission.

> 是三种基群-弦冠粒子，具有相同的性质和电荷排斥相互作用。当它们带电时，电子竞争保持垂直于z轴的方向，并通过三色弦彼此排斥，形成称作π粒子的三色弦核。π粒子由三个夸克和三个弦耦合而成，第四个反夸克则是在衰变过程中被创造出来。这些粒子负责制备某些元素的辉光，例如氢和铅，但不能是碳或氧。它们既可以利用核聚变生成，也可以利用核裂变产生。

## Valence Shell（电势带）
- A region of space inside an atom where the electrons that make up the atom reside. Normally, there are several types of electrons with varying amounts of free spin, number of orbits around the nucleus, and momentum direction relative to the motion of the nucleus. The greater the amount of spin carried by the electrons, the less likely they are to split into smaller fragments upon collision with other atoms. The valence shell is what allows an atom to retain stability and maintain its electronic and nuclear characteristics throughout the course of the universe's history.

> 电势带是指原子内部的一个空间区域，里面放着原子的电子。通常情况下，原子内会有各种不同数量、质量和角动量的电子，这些电子按照不同配置分布在原子核周围的不同位置。高旋度的电子会降低它们与其他原子发生碰撞后导致自身分裂的可能性。由于电势带能维持原子的稳定性和电子、核的特性，所以它一直扮演着至关重要的角色，使得整个宇宙的历史充满了奇妙的魔法效果。
