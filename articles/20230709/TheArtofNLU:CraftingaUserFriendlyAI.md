Technical Principles and Concepts
------------------------------

2.1. Basic Concepts
---------------

Before diving into the technical aspects of NLU, it is essential to understand some fundamental concepts.

* **Natural Language Understanding (NLU)**: NLU refers to the ability of computers to understand and interpret human language. It involves tasks such as text classification, named entity recognition, and dependency parsing.
* **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**: NLP refers to the branch of AI that focuses on enabling computers to process and understand human language. It involves tasks such as text completion, text summarization, and sentiment analysis.
* **Language Model**: A language model is a mathematical representation of the probability distribution of a language as a sequence of tokens. It enables computers to understand the context and meaning of words in a sentence.
* **Probability**: Probability is a measure of the likelihood of an event occurring. In the context of NLU, probability is used to calculate the likelihood of a word or phrase in a given sentence.

2.2. Technical Implementation
----------------------------

To create an effective NLU system, it is important to have a solid understanding of the underlying technical principles. This involves developing a robust set of data structures and algorithms that can effectively process natural language data.

It enables computers to understand the context and meaning of words in a sentence. To create a language model, one needs to have a large corpus of text data, a sufficient amount of training data, and an appropriate algorithm to train the model.

Another important component of an NLU system is theProbability Theory. Probability is a measure of the likelihood of an event occurring, and it is used to calculate the likelihood of a word or phrase in a given sentence. To calculate the probability of a word, one needs to compare the frequency of that word in the training data with the frequency of all words in the training data.
