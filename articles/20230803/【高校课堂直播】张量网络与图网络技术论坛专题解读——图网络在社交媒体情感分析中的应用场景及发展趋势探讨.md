         # 2. 相关知识背景介绍
         ## 2.1 张量网络(Tensor Network)
         ### 2.1.1 张量网络定义
         在古典力学中，张量是一个用来描述物质系统的二阶或三阶张量，它既有位置和空间维度，也具有旋转和其他指标。张量网络是指由多个张量构成的网络结构。张量网络最早由微分几何和数学物理学家李约瑟·康德于1954年提出，并基于微积分语言理论和代数群研究而成。
         
         ### 2.1.2 张量网络的作用
         张量网络是指由张量构成的数学网络结构，其特点是将复杂、动态、多维、非欧氏空间的物理变量映射为一组形式简单但功能丰富的线性组合，从而实现信息的快速、准确、高效地传输、存储、检索和利用。张量网络的设计思想主要有三个方面：
         1. 张量网络通过张量运算来有效整合来自不同领域的信息，并且可以有效处理海量的数据；
         2. 张量网络的广义坐标表示法能够有效地表示一个张量网络中所有节点之间的相互依赖关系，从而避免了常规的坐标表示法易受制于维度过高的问题；
         3. 张量网络的稀疏化特性使得它们可以适应各种不同的应用环境，并可以自动发现、利用数据的多种模式。
         ### 2.1.3 张量网络的分类
         张量网络按张量结构和物理意义划分，一般可以分为两种类型：
         1. 第一类张量网络是指对称性张量网络，它由对称性张量构成，这些张量均被表示为两个矩阵之和；
         2. 第二类张量网络是指非对称性张量网络，它由非对称性张量构成，这些张量中不全都是对称阵列。
         
         ## 2.2 图神经网络(Graph Neural Networks, GNNs)
         ### 2.2.1 图神经网络定义
         图神经网络是一种无监督学习方法，它可以直接从图结构中学习到图的特征。图神经网络借鉴了生物神经网络的原理，利用图的结构来模拟生物神经元网络，对节点的特征和边缘的关系进行学习。图神经网络的基本元素是图，它通过节点和边的邻接矩阵来描述。
         
         ### 2.2.2 图神经网络的作用
         图神经网络的主要优点如下：
         1. 图神经网络可以利用图结构的特点对图数据进行编码，提取出重要的特征；
         2. 图神经网络可以通过端到端训练的方式同时优化整个网络；
         3. 图神经网络的学习速度快且精度高，而且不容易陷入局部最小值或震荡。
         
         ### 2.2.3 图神经网络的应用
         目前图神经网络已经在社交媒体情感分析、推荐系统、生物信息学、金融领域得到广泛应用。在社交媒体情感分析中，图神经网络可以捕获文本、图像、视频等多种特征的组合信息，对微博、推特等新型社交媒体平台上的用户情感进行实时的分析；在推荐系统中，图神经网络可以从大量的物品和用户组成的图中提取特征向量，对用户进行个性化推荐；在生物信息学中，图神经网络可以利用蛋白质-蛋白质相互作用的结构关系、基因表达信号之间的关联关系等，对疾病的生物机理进行分析；在金融领域，图神经网络可以对股市的走势进行预测、对用户行为进行分析等。
         
         ### 2.2.4 图神经网络的发展方向
         图神经网络还有很多需要改进和完善的地方。近年来，随着图神经网络的兴起，人们对如何更好地学习图数据展开了长足的发展，如图学习、混合图学习、知识图谱等。此外，由于图结构数据具有动态、变化和不规则性，因此图神经网络还需要进一步适应动态图数据。
         
         # 3. 核心技术原理和操作步骤
         ## 3.1 概念理解
         ### 3.1.1 定义
         - **Social media sentiment analysis**: Social media sentiment analysis is the task of determining a person's attitude towards some topic by examining their interactions with social media platforms and analyzing what people have said or shared about it. In this paper, we will discuss how to apply graph neural networks for social media sentiment analysis.
         
         - **Graph network**: A graph network (GNN) is a type of artificial neural network that models relationships between objects in a complex system as nodes and edges connecting them in a graph structure. Graphs are commonly used to model relational data such as social networks or collaborations, where each object is represented as a node and connections are represented as directed links between nodes. In this paper, we will explore the application of graph neural networks on social media sentiment analysis tasks.
         
         - **Sentiment polarity labelling**: Sentiment polarity labelling refers to the process of classifying text into positive, negative, or neutral categories based on its underlying emotional tone. It plays an important role in various natural language processing applications, including sentiment analysis, opinion mining, machine translation, and content moderation. This approach has been widely adopted in the field of social media sentiment analysis because users express their feelings about topics they share on social media platforms using text messages and posts.
         
         - **Word embeddings**: Word embeddings are dense vectors representing individual words or phrases in a fixed-length vector space. The word embedding technique maps each word to a high-dimensional vector space where similar words are closer together while dissimilar words are farther apart. These dense representations can be learned from large corpora of texts and then applied to downstream NLP tasks like sentiment analysis, named entity recognition, and question answering.
         
         - **Sentiment lexicons**: Sentiment lexicons are dictionaries containing associations between words and their respective sentiment polarities. They provide a quick and easy way to assign sentiment labels to sentences or documents without requiring extensive linguistic knowledge. Common sentiment lexicons include the Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SST), which contains annotatons for movie reviews, and the Opinion Lexicon, which contains annotations for product reviews.
         
         - **Lexicon-based approaches**: Lexicon-based approaches use pre-defined lists of positive, negative, and neutral terms to classify tweets into sentiment polarity classes. This method is simple but may not accurately capture the nuances of human emotions and consequently may lead to biased results. Another challenge with lexicon-based methods is that they require manual construction of lexicons that are often limited in size and cover only a small subset of the available sentiment vocabulary.
         
         - **Deep learning-based approaches**: Deep learning-based approaches leverage deep learning architectures to automatically extract features from textual data, learn patterns through supervised training, and make predictions on new, unseen data points. Popular deep learning techniques include recurrent neural networks (RNNs), convolutional neural networks (CNNs), long short-term memory (LSTM) cells, and transformers. While these models offer state-of-the-art performance in many domains, they typically require significant amounts of labeled data and computational resources for training.
         
         - **Transformer-based approaches**: Transformers are attention mechanisms that allow the architecture to focus on relevant parts of the input sequence at every time step during decoding. These models have achieved impressive results in natural language processing tasks, including machine translation, text summarization, speech recognition, and question answering. Despite their success, building transformer-based models for sentiment analysis requires more computational resources than other deep learning models, making them less suitable for real-time applications on social media streams.

        ## 3.2 任务目标
         Our main task is to develop a pipeline for social media sentiment analysis using graph neural networks. Specifically, we aim to perform sentiment classification on a variety of social media platforms using both raw text data and processed feature vectors obtained using word embeddings. To evaluate our approach, we will use metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. To handle the dynamic nature of online social media streams, we will implement batch-based incremental learning techniques, such as stochastic gradient descent (SGD), which adaptively updates parameters of the graph neural network based on newly arrived data samples. Finally, we will experiment with several variants of our proposed pipeline and compare their performance against baseline models.
