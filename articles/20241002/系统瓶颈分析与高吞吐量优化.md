## Background

In the context of rapid advancements in information technology, the demand for data processing and transmission continues to grow. As a result, system performance optimization has become a critical issue across various industries. System bottlenecks often act as significant constraints on improving system capabilities, especially in high-concurrency and large-scale data processing scenarios. This article aims to delve into the analysis of system bottlenecks and high-throughput optimization strategies to provide readers with a comprehensive understanding of how to resolve performance bottlenecks and enhance overall system throughput.

A system bottleneck refers to a performance limitation within a system that prevents it from fully utilizing hardware resources and network bandwidth, thereby affecting the system's response time and processing capacity. Bottlenecks can arise from various factors, including insufficient hardware resources, complex software algorithms, poor database query efficiency, and network latency. Different types of bottlenecks require distinct optimization strategies to address effectively.
