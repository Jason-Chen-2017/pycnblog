# 2.基本概念术语说明：--------下一步，我们需要对NLP中的一些重要术语进行定义并简单说明一下其含义。请读者仔细阅读并理解以下内容：

1）What is natural language processing?

Natural language processing (NLP) is a subfield of artificial intelligence (AI) concerned with developing machines that can understand and manipulate human languages naturally, rather than relying on explicit programming instructions. Its goal is to enable computers to understand spoken words, sentences, and paragraphs, and interact with people in natural ways. 

The ability to interpret and process human language is essential to a wide range of tasks, from speech recognition to information retrieval and indexing. For example, search engines use natural language processing techniques to extract relevant information from billions of webpages. Chatbots are becoming increasingly popular as well, which engage users through conversations with artificial intelligence agents that imitate human conversation styles. 

2）Types of textual data

Textual data refers to any sequence of characters, symbols, or words that can be presented as written or spoken. There are three types of textual data commonly encountered in NLP:
1. Raw text: Text data collected directly from sources like emails, online reviews, social media platforms, customer feedback forms, etc. These texts can be classified based on their format, i.e., plain text, HTML, XML, JSON, etc. 
2. Structured documents: Documents containing predefined templates or layouts, typically in PDF or Word format, which contain both text and annotations. Examples include legal contracts, medical records, financial statements, and scientific papers.
3. Social media posts: Posts made on social media sites like Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etc., which often contain multimedia content like images, videos, and links.

All these textual data come under the broader category of unstructured text data, which requires additional processing steps to derive meaningful insights.


3）Tokenization

Tokenization involves splitting the input text into individual tokens or terms based on certain criteria, such as punctuation marks, whitespace, stopwords, or non-alphanumeric characters. Tokens are important because they capture the meaning of the sentence by representing each concept as a standalone unit. This makes it easier to perform downstream tasks like part-of-speech tagging, stemming, lemmatization, spell correction, and named entity recognition. 

4）Part-of-speech tagging

Part-of-speech tagging (POS tagging) assigns a part of speech tag (noun, verb, adjective, adverb, etc.) to each token in the sentence. This helps identify the role of each word in the context of the overall sentence. Part-of-speech tags play a crucial role in many natural language processing tasks, such as named entity recognition, question answering, and information extraction.

5）Named entity recognition

Named entity recognition (NER) identifies entities mentioned in text, such as persons, organizations, locations, times, and percentages, and labels them appropriately. It’s one of the most critical NLP tasks that enables us to extract valuable insights from complex sentences and text corpora. 

For example, given the following sentence "Apple is looking at buying a Macbook", NER could label "Apple" as an organization and "Macbook" as a product name.
