## 3. Algorithm Principles and Specific Operational Steps, Mathematical Models and Formulas

The algorithm for bank card binding typically involves several steps:

### 3.1 Authentication

The first step in bank card binding is authentication. Users must provide their bank card information, such as card number, expiration date, and CVV code, to authenticate their identity.

### 3.2 Authorization

After authentication, users must authorize the bank card binding request. This process typically involves entering a one-time password (OTP) sent to their registered mobile number or email address.

### 3.3 Tokenization

Once authorized, the bank card information is tokenized, meaning that it's converted into a unique identifier called a token. The token is then stored securely on the payment channel's server.

### 3.4 Verification

Finally, the payment channel verifies the token with the bank's server to ensure that the bank card is valid and has sufficient funds.

The mathematical model for bank card binding can be represented as follows:

$$Token = f(CardNumber, ExpirationDate, CVVCode)$$

where f() represents the tokenization function, CardNumber is the bank card number, ExpirationDate is the bank card's expiration date, and CVVCode is the card verification value.
