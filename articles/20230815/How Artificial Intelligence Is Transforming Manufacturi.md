# 2. 基础知识和术语介绍
## 2.1 什么是AI
Artificial intelligence (AI) is the ability of machines to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence such as learning and problem-solving. AI involves designing computer programs that can think like humans or mimic their behavior. These programs are able to learn from experience and make decisions based on algorithms that emulate the way a human brain works. 

There are three types of artificial intelligence: symbolic intelligence, statistical intelligence, and neural network intelligence.

### Symbolic Intelligent Systems (SIS):
These systems use logical rules and reasoning to solve problems. They often have an underlying logic and can recognize patterns in data without explicitly programming them. Examples include expert systems, rule engines, and knowledge bases.

Symbolic intelligent systems usually operate by processing input data through a set of predefined rules and procedures, known as inference engine. The output is determined by the outcome of this process.

### Statistical Intelligent Systems (SIS):
These systems rely on probability distributions, mathematical formulas, and pattern recognition techniques for decision making. They analyze large amounts of data and predict outcomes based on patterns found within it. Examples include machine learning, natural language processing, and speech recognition.

Statistical intelligent systems work by analyzing historical data to identify patterns and trends that may be useful for prediction purposes. Based on these predictions, they generate new insights into future outcomes.

### Neural Network Intelligence (NIS):
Neural networks are mathematical models inspired by the structure and function of the human brain’s neurons. NIS is built using layers of interconnected nodes that process input data to produce outputs. Examples include deep learning, computer vision, and reinforcement learning.

In recent years, the popularity of neural networks has increased dramatically due to their effectiveness at solving complex problems. With advancements in hardware technology and big data storage capacity, neural networks can now handle massive volumes of data with ease.

Neural networks can also learn from mistakes made during training, allowing them to adapt to changing circumstances and improve performance over time. Furthermore, they enable faster detection of anomalies than traditional methods.

## 2.2 机器学习、深度学习和强化学习
A key aspect of AI is its ability to learn from experience. There are several machine learning approaches that aim to teach computers how to effectively solve problems. Here's what each one does:

**Supervised Learning:** This type of learning involves feeding the algorithm labeled examples of inputs and expected outputs. It learns by comparing the actual results produced by the model to the expected results and adjusting itself accordingly until it produces accurate results. Supervised learning is used when there is a clear relationship between the inputs and outputs.

**Unsupervised Learning:** This type of learning involves feeding the algorithm unlabeled data and attempting to discover patterns or relationships within it. Unsupervised learning can find clusters of similar data points, identifying groups of customers who purchase products together, or detecting anomalous behavior that could indicate fraudulent activity.

**Reinforcement Learning:** Reinforcement learning is another approach where an agent interacts with an environment and receives feedback in the form of rewards or punishments for its actions. The goal is to maximize the long-term reward by finding the optimal sequence of actions that yield maximum rewards. Reinforcement learning is commonly used in games, robotics, and autonomous vehicles.

**Deep Learning:** Deep learning is a subset of machine learning that uses multi-layered neural networks to learn complex functions from large datasets. It is widely used in image recognition, speech recognition, and natural language processing. Deep learning allows computers to understand high-dimensional data, which makes it more efficient and effective at extracting valuable information from vast amounts of data.

Another important technique used for AI is **Natural Language Processing**, which involves converting text into a format that can be understood by computers. The goal of Natural Language Processing (NLP) is to create software that can read, understand, and manipulate human languages.

In summary, the main types of machine learning used in AI are supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning. Deep learning provides advanced features for processing large datasets, while Natural Language Processing enables natural communication between devices.
