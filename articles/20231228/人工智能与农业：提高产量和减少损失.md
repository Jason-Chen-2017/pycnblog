## 1. Background Introduction

The agricultural industry is facing several challenges, including:

- Declining arable land: Due to urbanization and environmental degradation, the amount of arable land available for farming is decreasing.
- Climate change: Extreme weather events, such as droughts and floods, are becoming more frequent and severe, affecting crop yields.
- Pests and diseases: The spread of pests and diseases is accelerating, threatening food security.
- Labor shortage: The aging population and migration of young people to cities have led to a shortage of agricultural labor.

To address these challenges, farmers and researchers are turning to AI and big data to improve agricultural productivity and reduce losses. AI can help in various ways, such as:

- Precision agriculture: Using sensors and data analysis to optimize crop management, such as irrigation, fertilization, and pest control.
- Crop disease and pest detection: Identifying diseases and pests early and taking appropriate action to minimize damage.
- Yield prediction: Predicting crop yields based on historical data and environmental factors to optimize resource allocation.
- Autonomous farming: Automating farming tasks, such as planting, harvesting, and transportation, to reduce labor costs and increase efficiency.
