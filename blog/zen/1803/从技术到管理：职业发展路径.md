Before exploring the career development path from technology to management, we need to clarify the essential differences between the two roles.

**Technical roles** focus on solving specific technical problems, which involve programming, system design, performance optimization, and more. Technical professionals typically focus on details, pursuing code efficiency, readability, and reliability.

**Management roles**, on the other hand, focus on coordinating and guiding teams to achieve organizational goals. Managers need to have strategic planning, decision-making capabilities, communication skills, and leadership. Management is not just about executing tasks but also about influencing and leading a team.

### 2.2 The Process of Transitioning from Technology to Management

1. **Self-awareness**: Technical professionals need to recognize their interests and abilities to determine if they are suitable for a transition to a managerial role.
2. **Learning Management Knowledge**: Through attending training courses, reading management books, and articles, understand the basic theories and practical methods of management.
3. **Accumulating Experience**: By participating in project management and team leadership roles, accumulate management experience.
4. **Improving Leadership**: Through self-improvement and team building, cultivate leadership and influence.
5. **Career Planning**: Clearly define career goals and develop practical plans, gradually realizing them.

### 2.3 The Connection Between Technology and Management
