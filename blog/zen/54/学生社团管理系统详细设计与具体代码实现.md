## 4. Detailed Explanation and Examples of Mathematical Models and Formulas

This section will provide a detailed explanation of the mathematical models and formulas used in the SAMS, along with practical examples.

### 4.1 Member Management Mathematical Models and Formulas

#### 4.1.1 Member Registration Formula

The member registration formula calculates the number of new members registered during a specific period.

$$
\text{New Members} = \text{Total Registrations} - \text{Cancelled Registrations}
$$

#### 4.1.2 Member Status Management Formula

The member status management formula calculates the percentage of active members in the association.

$$
\text{Active Members Percentage} = \frac{\text{Active Members}}{\text{Total Members}} \times 100\%
$$

### 4.2 Event Management Mathematical Models and Formulas

#### 4.2.1 Event Registration Formula

The event registration formula calculates the number of attendees for a specific event.

$$
\text{Attendees} = \text{Total Registrations} - \text{No-Shows}
$$

#### 4.2.2 Event Feedback Formula

The event feedback formula calculates the average rating for a specific event.

$$
\text{Average Rating} = \frac{\sum\text{Ratings}}{\text{Total Ratings}}
$$

### 4.3 Financial Management Mathematical Models and Formulas

#### 4.3.1 Income Generation Formula

The income generation formula calculates the total income generated during a specific period.

$$
\text{Total Income} = \text{Donations} + \text{Sponsorships} + \text{Event Revenue}
$$

#### 4.3.2 Expense Tracking Formula

The expense tracking formula calculates the total expenses incurred during a specific period.

$$
\text{Total Expenses} = \text{Operating Costs} + \text{Event Costs}
$$

### 4.4 Communication Mathematical Models and Formulas

#### 4.4.1 Email Delivery Formula

The email delivery formula calculates the percentage of emails delivered successfully.

$$
\text{Delivery Percentage} = \frac{\text{Delivered Emails}}{\text{Total Emails}} \times 100\%
$$
