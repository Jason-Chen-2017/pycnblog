**The Pythagorean Theorem**

One of Pythagoras's most famous contributions is the Pythagorean theorem, which states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the length of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides. This theorem is fundamental to computation and geometry.

### 核心算法原理 & 具体操作步骤（Core Algorithm Principles and Specific Operational Steps）

为了解决希帕索斯的悖论，数学家们开始研究无理数。无理数是不能表示为两个整数之比的数，例如π（圆周率）和√2（根号2）。这一研究揭示了数学的深层次结构。

**The Study of Irrational Numbers**

To resolve Hippasos's paradox, mathematicians began to study irrational numbers. Irrational numbers are numbers that cannot be expressed as a ratio of two integers, such as π (pi) and √2 (the square root of 2). This study revealed deeper structures within mathematics.

**Proof of the Irrationality of √2**

一个著名的证明无理数的方法是证明√2是无理数。我们使用反证法：

假设√2是有理数，可以表示为两个整数a和b的比，其中a和b没有公因数，即它们互质。那么我们有：

$$ √2 = \frac{a}{b} $$

平方两边得到：

$$ 2 = \frac{a^2}{b^2} $$

这意味着：

$$ a^2 = 2b^2 $$

这意味着a的平方是偶数，因此a也是偶数。我们可以将a写成2c的形式，其中c是整数。然后我们得到：

$$ (2c)^2 = 2b^2 $$

$$ 4c^2 = 2b^2 $$

$$ 2c^2 = b^2 $$

这意味着b的平方也是偶数，因此b也是偶数。这与我们之前的假设a和b互质相矛盾。因此，我们的假设是错误的，√2不是有理数。

**Proof of the Irrationality of √2**

A well-known method for proving the irrationality of √2 is to use proof by contradiction. We assume that √2 is rational, which can be expressed as a fraction of two integers a and b, with no common factors (i.e., they are coprime). We have:

$$ √2 = \frac{a}{b} $$

Squaring both sides, we get:

$$ 2 = \frac{a^2}{b^2} $$

This implies:

$$ a^2 = 2b^2 $$

This means that a^2 is even, and therefore, a is even. We can express a as 2c, where c is an integer. Then we have:

$$ (2c)^2 = 2b^2 $$

$$ 4c^2 = 2b^2 $$

$$ 2c^2 = b^2 $$

This means that b^2 is also even, and therefore, b is even. This contradicts our initial assumption that a and b are coprime. Therefore, our assumption is false, and √2 is not rational.

In this example, we used the Pythagorean theorem to calculate the length of the hypotenuse of a right-angled triangle. The theorem tells us that the length of the hypotenuse is equal to the square root of the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides.
