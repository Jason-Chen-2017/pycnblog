在创业过程中，贾扬清运用了一系列数学模型和公式来支持他的决策和战略规划。这些模型和公式不仅帮助他准确地评估企业的发展状况，还为他的领导决策提供了科学依据。以下是几个关键数学模型和公式的详细讲解与举例说明。

#### 4.1 成本效益分析（Cost-Benefit Analysis）

成本效益分析是一种评估决策成本和预期效益的方法，用于确定资源分配的最优方案。公式如下：

\[ \text{成本效益比} = \frac{\text{预期效益}}{\text{总成本}} \]

举例说明：在决定是否投资某个新项目时，贾扬清会计算项目的预期收益和所需投入成本，然后计算成本效益比。如果成本效益比大于1，则项目具有经济效益，可以考虑投资；否则，应重新评估项目可行性。

#### 4.2 SWOT分析（SWOT Analysis）

SWOT分析是一种用于评估企业内部优势和劣势、外部机会和威胁的方法。公式如下：

\[ \text{SWOT} = (\text{Strengths}, \text{Weaknesses}, \text{Opportunities}, \text{Threats}) \]

举例说明：贾扬清在制定战略规划时，会通过SWOT分析来确定旷视科技的优势、劣势、机会和威胁。例如，旷视科技的优势可能包括技术领先、团队优秀等，而劣势可能包括市场认知度低、资金不足等。通过分析这些因素，贾扬清可以制定相应的策略来发挥优势、克服劣势、抓住机会和应对威胁。

#### 4.3 利润最大化模型（Profit Maximization Model）

利润最大化模型是一种用于确定最优定价和产量组合以实现利润最大化的方法。公式如下：

\[ \text{利润} = (\text{单价} \times \text{销量}) - (\text{固定成本} + \text{可变成本}) \]

举例说明：在确定产品定价和产量时，贾扬清会根据市场需求、成本结构和竞争对手的定价策略，运用利润最大化模型来计算最优定价和产量。例如，如果市场需求较高，他可能会提高产品价格以增加利润；如果市场需求较低，他可能会降低价格以增加销量。

#### 4.4 机会成本模型（Opportunity Cost Model）

机会成本模型是一种用于评估放弃某种选择所导致的潜在损失的方法。公式如下：

\[ \text{机会成本} = \text{其他最佳选择} \]

举例说明：在面临多个投资机会时，贾扬清会评估每个机会的潜在收益和风险，然后计算每个机会的机会成本。例如，如果他决定投资一个新项目，那么这个决定的机会成本就是放弃其他投资机会所导致的潜在收益损失。

通过运用这些数学模型和公式，贾扬清能够更准确地评估企业的发展状况和决策效果，从而提高企业的竞争力和盈利能力。

#### 4.1 Cost-Benefit Analysis

Cost-benefit analysis is a method used to assess the costs and expected benefits of a decision, helping to determine the optimal allocation of resources. The formula is as follows:

\[ \text{Cost-Benefit Ratio} = \frac{\text{Expected Benefits}}{\text{Total Costs}} \]

Example: When deciding whether to invest in a new project, Yangqing would calculate the expected revenue and the required investment costs, then compute the cost-benefit ratio. If the cost-benefit ratio is greater than 1, the project is considered economically viable, and investment can be considered; otherwise, the feasibility of the project should be re-evaluated.

#### 4.2 SWOT Analysis

SWOT analysis is a method used to evaluate a company's internal strengths and weaknesses, as well as external opportunities and threats. The formula is as follows:

\[ \text{SWOT} = (\text{Strengths}, \text{Weaknesses}, \text{Opportunities}, \text{Threats}) \]

Example: When developing a strategic plan, Yangqing would perform a SWOT analysis to determine Megvii's strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats. For instance, Megvii's strengths might include technological leadership and a talented team, while weaknesses might include low market recognition and limited funds. By analyzing these factors, Yangqing can develop strategies to leverage strengths, overcome weaknesses, seize opportunities, and mitigate threats.

#### 4.3 Profit Maximization Model

The profit maximization model is a method used to determine the optimal pricing and production combinations to achieve maximum profit. The formula is as follows:

\[ \text{Profit} = (\text{Price} \times \text{Quantity Sold}) - (\text{Fixed Costs} + \text{Variable Costs}) \]

Example: When determining product pricing and quantity, Yangqing would consider market demand, cost structures, and competitors' pricing strategies using the profit maximization model to calculate the optimal pricing and production. For example, if market demand is high, he might increase the product price to boost profits; if market demand is low, he might decrease the price to increase sales.

#### 4.4 Opportunity Cost Model

The opportunity cost model is a method used to assess the potential loss incurred by forgoing a certain choice. The formula is as follows:

\[ \text{Opportunity Cost} = \text{Best Alternative} \]

Example: When facing multiple investment opportunities, Yangqing would evaluate the potential returns and risks of each opportunity and compute the opportunity cost. For instance, if he decides to invest in a new project, the opportunity cost would be the potential gains from forgoing other investment opportunities.
