### 4. 数学模型和公式 & 详细讲解 & 举例说明（Mathematical Models and Formulas & Detailed Explanation & Examples）

在电商平台供给链管理中，数学模型和公式是核心的工具。以下我们将介绍一些常用的数学模型和公式，包括时间序列模型、回归模型和优化模型，并给出详细讲解和举例说明。

#### 4.1 时间序列模型（Time Series Models）

时间序列模型用于分析历史数据，预测未来的趋势。以下是两种常见的时间序列模型：移动平均模型（Moving Average Model）和指数平滑模型（Exponential Smoothing Model）。

**移动平均模型（Moving Average Model）**

移动平均模型通过计算一定时间窗口内的平均值来预测未来的趋势。其公式如下：

\[ \hat{y_t} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} y_{t-i}}{n} \]

其中，\( \hat{y_t} \) 是第 \( t \) 期的预测值，\( y_{t-i} \) 是第 \( t-i \) 期的实际值，\( n \) 是移动平均窗口的长度。

**指数平滑模型（Exponential Smoothing Model）**

指数平滑模型通过给历史数据不同的权重来预测未来的趋势。其公式如下：

\[ \hat{y_t} = \alpha y_{t-1} + (1 - \alpha) \hat{y_{t-1}} \]

其中，\( \hat{y_t} \) 是第 \( t \) 期的预测值，\( y_{t-1} \) 是第 \( t-1 \) 期的实际值，\( \hat{y_{t-1}} \) 是第 \( t-1 \) 期的预测值，\( \alpha \) 是平滑系数，取值范围在 \( 0 \) 到 \( 1 \) 之间。

**举例说明**

假设某电商平台的月销售额历史数据如下表所示：

| 月份 | 销售额（万元） |
| ---- | ------------- |
| 1    | 30           |
| 2    | 35           |
| 3    | 40           |
| 4    | 38           |
| 5    | 42           |

使用3个月移动平均模型预测第6个月的销售额。

计算过程如下：

\[ \hat{y_6} = \frac{30 + 35 + 40}{3} = 37 \]

预测第6个月的销售额为37万元。

#### 4.2 回归模型（Regression Models）

回归模型用于分析两个或多个变量之间的关系，预测一个变量的值。线性回归模型是其中最简单的一种，其公式如下：

\[ y = \beta_0 + \beta_1 x + \epsilon \]

其中，\( y \) 是因变量，\( x \) 是自变量，\( \beta_0 \) 和 \( \beta_1 \) 是回归系数，\( \epsilon \) 是误差项。

**举例说明**

假设某电商平台的月销售额（\( y \)）与月广告投入（\( x \)）之间存在线性关系。给定以下数据：

| 月份 | 广告投入（万元） | 销售额（万元） |
| ---- | ---------------- | ------------- |
| 1    | 5               | 30           |
| 2    | 7               | 35           |
| 3    | 10              | 40           |
| 4    | 8               | 38           |
| 5    | 12              | 42           |

使用线性回归模型预测当月广告投入为10万元时的销售额。

首先，计算回归系数：

\[ \beta_0 = \bar{y} - \beta_1 \bar{x} \]

\[ \beta_1 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i - \bar{x})(y_i - \bar{y})}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i - \bar{x})^2} \]

其中，\( \bar{y} \) 和 \( \bar{x} \) 分别是销售额和广告投入的平均值，\( n \) 是数据点的数量。

计算过程如下：

\[ \bar{y} = \frac{30 + 35 + 40 + 38 + 42}{5} = 38 \]

\[ \bar{x} = \frac{5 + 7 + 10 + 8 + 12}{5} = 8.4 \]

\[ \beta_1 = \frac{(5-8.4)(30-38) + (7-8.4)(35-38) + (10-8.4)(40-38) + (8-8.4)(38-38) + (12-8.4)(42-38)}{(5-8.4)^2 + (7-8.4)^2 + (10-8.4)^2 + (8-8.4)^2 + (12-8.4)^2} \]

\[ \beta_1 = \frac{(-3.4)(-8) + (-1.4)(-3) + (1.6)(2) + (-0.4)(0) + (3.6)(4)}{(-3.4)^2 + (-1.4)^2 + (1.6)^2 + (-0.4)^2 + (3.6)^2} \]

\[ \beta_1 = \frac{27.2 + 4.2 + 3.2 + 0 + 14.4}{11.56 + 1.96 + 2.56 + 0.16 + 12.96} \]

\[ \beta_1 = \frac{48.0}{38.2} \]

\[ \beta_1 \approx 1.25 \]

\[ \beta_0 = 38 - 1.25 \times 8.4 \]

\[ \beta_0 = 38 - 10.5 \]

\[ \beta_0 = 27.5 \]

然后，使用回归模型预测当月广告投入为10万元时的销售额：

\[ \hat{y} = 27.5 + 1.25 \times 10 \]

\[ \hat{y} = 37.5 \]

预测当月广告投入为10万元时的销售额为37.5万元。

#### 4.3 优化模型（Optimization Models）

优化模型用于确定最优的决策变量，以实现某个目标函数的最大化或最小化。线性规划模型是其中最常用的一种，其公式如下：

\[ \max \quad c^T x \]

\[ \text{subject to} \quad Ax \leq b \]

\[ x \geq 0 \]

其中，\( c \) 是系数向量，\( x \) 是决策变量向量，\( A \) 是系数矩阵，\( b \) 是常数向量。

**举例说明**

假设某电商平台需要确定最优的库存水平和补货策略，以实现总成本的最小化。给定以下约束条件：

1. 库存容量限制：\( x_1 + x_2 \leq 100 \)

2. 采购成本限制：\( 2x_1 + 3x_2 \leq 200 \)

3. 库存持有成本限制：\( x_1 + 2x_2 \leq 150 \)

4. 库存水平非负：\( x_1, x_2 \geq 0 \)

目标函数：最小化总成本 \( c^T x \)，其中 \( c = [1, 1] \)。

使用线性规划模型求解最优解。

构建线性规划模型如下：

\[ \min \quad x_1 + x_2 \]

\[ \text{subject to} \quad x_1 + x_2 \leq 100 \]

\[ 2x_1 + 3x_2 \leq 200 \]

\[ x_1 + 2x_2 \leq 150 \]

\[ x_1, x_2 \geq 0 \]

使用单纯形法求解该线性规划模型。求解结果为 \( x_1 = 50 \)，\( x_2 = 50 \)，总成本为100。

### Mathematical Models and Formulas & Detailed Explanation & Examples

In e-commerce supply chain management, mathematical models and formulas are core tools. Below, we introduce some commonly used mathematical models and formulas, including time series models, regression models, and optimization models, along with detailed explanations and examples.

#### 4.1 Time Series Models

Time series models are used to analyze historical data and predict future trends. Here are two common time series models: the moving average model and the exponential smoothing model.

**Moving Average Model**

The moving average model predicts future trends by calculating the average of a fixed time window. Its formula is as follows:

\[ \hat{y_t} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} y_{t-i}}{n} \]

Where \( \hat{y_t} \) is the predicted value for period \( t \), \( y_{t-i} \) is the actual value for period \( t-i \), and \( n \) is the length of the moving average window.

**Exponential Smoothing Model**

The exponential smoothing model predicts future trends by assigning different weights to historical data. Its formula is as follows:

\[ \hat{y_t} = \alpha y_{t-1} + (1 - \alpha) \hat{y_{t-1}} \]

Where \( \hat{y_t} \) is the predicted value for period \( t \), \( y_{t-1} \) is the actual value for period \( t-1 \), \( \hat{y_{t-1}} \) is the predicted value for period \( t-1 \), and \( \alpha \) is the smoothing coefficient, which ranges from 0 to 1.

**Example**

Assume that the monthly sales data for an e-commerce platform is as follows:

| Month | Sales (Ten thousand yuan) |
| ---- | ------------------------ |
| 1    | 30                      |
| 2    | 35                      |
| 3    | 40                      |
| 4    | 38                      |
| 5    | 42                      |

Use the 3-month moving average model to predict the sales for the 6th month.

The calculation process is as follows:

\[ \hat{y_6} = \frac{30 + 35 + 40}{3} = 37 \]

The predicted sales for the 6th month is 37 thousand yuan.

#### 4.2 Regression Models

Regression models are used to analyze the relationships between two or more variables and predict the value of one variable. Linear regression models are the simplest type. Its formula is as follows:

\[ y = \beta_0 + \beta_1 x + \epsilon \]

Where \( y \) is the dependent variable, \( x \) is the independent variable, \( \beta_0 \) and \( \beta_1 \) are the regression coefficients, and \( \epsilon \) is the error term.

**Example**

Assume that there is a linear relationship between the monthly sales (\( y \)) and the monthly advertising expenditure (\( x \)) of an e-commerce platform. Given the following data:

| Month | Advertising Expenditure (Ten thousand yuan) | Sales (Ten thousand yuan) |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------ | ------------------------ |
| 1    | 5                                          | 30                      |
| 2    | 7                                          | 35                      |
| 3    | 10                                         | 40                      |
| 4    | 8                                          | 38                      |
| 5    | 12                                         | 42                      |

Use linear regression to predict the sales when the monthly advertising expenditure is 10 thousand yuan.

First, calculate the regression coefficients:

\[ \beta_0 = \bar{y} - \beta_1 \bar{x} \]

\[ \beta_1 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i - \bar{x})(y_i - \bar{y})}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i - \bar{x})^2} \]

Where \( \bar{y} \) and \( \bar{x} \) are the averages of sales and advertising expenditure, respectively, and \( n \) is the number of data points.

Calculation process:

\[ \bar{y} = \frac{30 + 35 + 40 + 38 + 42}{5} = 38 \]

\[ \bar{x} = \frac{5 + 7 + 10 + 8 + 12}{5} = 8.4 \]

\[ \beta_1 = \frac{(5-8.4)(30-38) + (7-8.4)(35-38) + (10-8.4)(40-38) + (8-8.4)(38-38) + (12-8.4)(42-38)}{(5-8.4)^2 + (7-8.4)^2 + (10-8.4)^2 + (8-8.4)^2 + (12-8.4)^2} \]

\[ \beta_1 = \frac{(-3.4)(-8) + (-1.4)(-3) + (1.6)(2) + (-0.4)(0) + (3.6)(4)}{(-3.4)^2 + (-1.4)^2 + (1.6)^2 + (-0.4)^2 + (3.6)^2} \]

\[ \beta_1 = \frac{27.2 + 4.2 + 3.2 + 0 + 14.4}{11.56 + 1.96 + 2.56 + 0.16 + 12.96} \]

\[ \beta_1 = \frac{48.0}{38.2} \]

\[ \beta_1 \approx 1.25 \]

\[ \beta_0 = 38 - 1.25 \times 8.4 \]

\[ \beta_0 = 38 - 10.5 \]

\[ \beta_0 = 27.5 \]

Then, use the regression model to predict the sales when the monthly advertising expenditure is 10 thousand yuan:

\[ \hat{y} = 27.5 + 1.25 \times 10 \]

\[ \hat{y} = 37.5 \]

The predicted sales when the monthly advertising expenditure is 10 thousand yuan is 37.5 thousand yuan.

#### 4.3 Optimization Models

Optimization models are used to determine the optimal decision variables to achieve the maximum or minimum of a given objective function. Linear programming models are the most commonly used. Its formula is as follows:

\[ \max \quad c^T x \]

\[ \text{subject to} \quad Ax \leq b \]

\[ x \geq 0 \]

Where \( c \) is the coefficient vector, \( x \) is the decision variable vector, \( A \) is the coefficient matrix, \( b \) is the constant vector.

**Example**

Assume that an e-commerce platform needs to determine the optimal inventory levels and replenishment strategies to minimize total costs. Given the following constraints:

1. Inventory capacity constraint: \( x_1 + x_2 \leq 100 \)
2. Procurement cost constraint: \( 2x_1 + 3x_2 \leq 200 \)
3. Inventory holding cost constraint: \( x_1 + 2x_2 \leq 150 \)
4. Non-negativity of inventory levels: \( x_1, x_2 \geq 0 \)

Objective function: Minimize total cost \( c^T x \), where \( c = [1, 1] \).

Use linear programming to solve for the optimal solution.

Construct the linear programming model as follows:

\[ \min \quad x_1 + x_2 \]

\[ \text{subject to} \quad x_1 + x_2 \leq 100 \]

\[ 2x_1 + 3x_2 \leq 200 \]

\[ x_1 + 2x_2 \leq 150 \]

\[ x_1, x_2 \geq 0 \]

Use the simplex method to solve this linear programming model. The optimal solution is \( x_1 = 50 \), \( x_2 = 50 \), with a total cost of 100.

import pandas as pd
from sklearn.linear_model import LinearRegression
from sklearn.metrics import mean_squared_error

# 加载数据
data = pd.read_csv('sales_data.csv')  # 假设数据文件名为sales_data.csv

regression_model = LinearRegression()
regression_model.fit(ma3.values.reshape(-1, 1), sales.values)

# 预测未来的销售额

plt.legend()
