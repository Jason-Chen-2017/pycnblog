## 4. Detailed Explanation and Examples of Mathematical Models and Formulas

### 4.1 PageRank Formula

The PageRank algorithm, developed by Google co-founder Larry Page, is a mathematical model used to determine the importance of a webpage based on its links.

$$PR(A) = (1 - d) + d \\sum_{j=1}^{n} \\frac{PR(j)}{L(j)}$$

Where:
- $PR(A)$ is the PageRank of page A
- $d$ is the damping factor (usually set to 0.85)
- $n$ is the total number of pages on the web
- $PR(j)$ is the PageRank of page j
- $L(j)$ is the number of outgoing links from page j

### 4.2 HITS Algorithm

The HITS (Hyperlink-Induced Topic Search) algorithm is another mathematical model used to rank webpages based on their relevance to a specific topic.

$$Authority(A) = \\sum_{B \\in In(A)} \\frac{H(B)}{Out(B)}$$

$$H(A) = \\sum_{B \\in Out(A)} \\frac{Authority(B)}{Out(B)}$$

Where:
- $Authority(A)$ is the authority score of page A
- $In(A)$ is the set of pages that link to page A
- $Out(A)$ is the set of outgoing links from page A
- $H(A)$ is the hub score of page A
